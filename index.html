Proposed South County DMV on Lyons Road: What Delray Beach and Ag Reserve Residents Need to Know

A comprehensive overview of the planned DMV/Tax Collector facility and the community concerns shaping the conversation

Edmund Bogen
Team Leader | The Edmund Bogen Team at Douglas Elliman
December 2025

A significant development is taking shape that could impact thousands of residents in West Delray Beach and the Agricultural Reserve. Palm Beach County has announced plans for a new South County Service Center—a DMV and Tax Collector facility—to be located on Lyons Road. As a Delray Beach resident and real estate professional serving our community, I want to provide you with a comprehensive overview of this project and the concerns being raised by local residents.

The Project at a Glance

Palm Beach County purchased approximately 4.2 acres on Lyons Road in late 2024 for $4.5 million. The proposed facility will span 23,735 to 24,000 square feet and is intended to replace the current Congress Avenue location, which county officials describe as undersized and outdated for the growing South County population.

Key Project Details

  • Land Purchase Late 2024 ($4.5 million)
  • Facility Size 23,735 - 24,000 sq ft
  • Groundbreaking February 2026
  • Projected Opening 2027
  • Construction Budget $4.25 million

Why the County Says This is Necessary

Tax Collector officials point to significant population growth in South Palm Beach County as the primary driver for this project. The existing Congress Avenue facility, they argue, can no longer adequately serve the expanding population. The Lyons Road location was selected for its proximity to major roadways, which county planners believe will improve accessibility for residents throughout the region.

Community Concerns

Residents from nearby communities have voiced strong opposition to the project, raising several key concerns:

Agricultural Reserve Preservation

The Agricultural Reserve was established with specific preservation goals in mind—to protect farmland and limit commercial development in western Palm Beach County. Many residents argue that a government service center of this size contradicts the founding principles of the Ag Reserve.

Traffic Impact

Traffic studies estimate the facility will generate between 482 and 500+ vehicle trips daily. For a region where many residents specifically chose to live for its quieter, less congested character, this projected traffic increase is a significant concern.

Property Values

As someone who closely monitors real estate trends in our market, I understand why homeowners worry about the potential impact on property values. The introduction of a high-traffic government facility can affect the desirability and character of surrounding residential areas.

What This Means for Homeowners

Whether you currently own property near the proposed site or are considering purchasing in the area, this development is worth monitoring. Changes to traffic patterns, the character of a neighborhood, and local infrastructure can all influence property values and quality of life.
